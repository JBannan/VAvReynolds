Plaintiff Witness: Redford Herring, Attorney General of the Commonwealth of Virginia

The Honourable Redford Herring, Attorney General of the Commonwealth of Virginia, is the acting power of attorney for the state government, and is seeking to prosecute the defendant on violations of the ‘TUBES’ (transmission of unsolicited bulk electronic mails) statute of the Virginia Computer Crimes Act.

Long before his election as a public official for the state government, he worked his way up after graduating with honors from the UVA School of Law by taking small clerking positions here and there, and then working as associate corporate counsel for several medium-sized companies, one of which happened to be CauliflowerOnline Corporation (then known by its founding name, Broccol-eMail, Inc.); this tenure lasted from 1996-2002. He continued in corporate law for a brief time after this before establishing his own practice, Red Herring Law Firm, P.C. in 2004, being the principal associate until he ventured into public office by being elected chief deputy attorney general by voters in a special election in 2010; he has been re-elected twice since then (in 2012 and 2016, respectively).

Pro-Plaintiff Knowledge:

Having a storied history as a law practitioner, Mr. Herring is knowledgeable regarding the state’s anti-spam laws; he interjected himself into the case on behalf of the state police whose cybercrimes division wanted to go after the defendant (Mr. Reynolds) after tracing the bulk emails back to him.

Pro-Defendant Knowledge:

Due to his publicized history with the CauliflowerOnline organization (albeit under its former name), he could present a potential conflict of interest that the defense could assert as justification for having the case thrown out.
